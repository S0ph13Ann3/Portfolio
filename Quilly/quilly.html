a digital sisterhood

College is marketed as the "best years of your life," yet for many women, it’s isolating. Quilly bridges this gap by creating a safe, authentic digital space to find community. I was a part of the team that led the design of the beta launch at UC Berkeley, prioritizing real-world connection, safety, and genuine support over vanity metrics.

The Challenge

"How might we design a femme-centered space that empowers college women to find their 'forever friends' and navigate campus life with less stress?"

Design Goals

  • Authentic Connection

    Prioritize real-world meetups and genuine support over likes and followers.

  • Safety First

    Ensure a verified, femme-centered environment where users feel secure.

  • Intuitive Support

    Design a 'pocket guide' interface that makes finding resources effortless.

context

The Mission

The rise of loneliness among college women has risen over the years and Quilly has made it our mission to create a real, supportive community — not just another social app or club. It offers a safe, authentic space where students can form meaningful connections and get the everyday support they need to feel less alone.

My Role

I joined Quilly as a UX Designer, leading the design efforts for the beta version of the app. My work involved integrating user research conducted prior to my arrival and closely partnering with the CEO to bring their design vision to life in a way that aligned with user needs and product goals.

Phase 1: Initial Concepts

In our first design iteration, we used house colors as the primary theme throughout the app, based on each user's assigned house. While this added a fun, personalized touch, the house colors often felt overpowering and didn't align with the clean, minimal aesthetic we were aiming for.

Phase 2: The Pivot

After user feedback, we realized many comments were about the color scheme. We decided to tone down the house colors and go for a more minimal and clean look. We also discussed adding a feature similar to Instagram's "Close Friends," where users can see what their besties are up to.

Phase 3: Final Polish

When presenting our initial design to the CEO, she noted that while we had achieved a more subtle color scheme, it still lacked the clean, fresh aesthetic she envisioned. This feedback sent us back to the drawing board, where our focus shifted to creating a truly simple, intuitive UI that prioritizes ample white space.

What's Next?

Our beta is now live for students at UC Berkeley and available for download on our website. This initial launch is a key step as we refine the app based on user feedback.

We have already seen significant interest, with over 400 students from other college campuses joining our waitlist for future launches.
