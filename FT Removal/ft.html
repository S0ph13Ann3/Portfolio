restoring control to facetime

FaceTime is celebrated for its seamless simplicity, but that simplicity often comes at the cost of control. Currently, when a group call becomes disruptive, the host is left powerless‚Äîforced to either endure the chaos or hang up on everyone.

I took the initiative to bridge this gap by designing a native moderation suite. The result is a more resilient communication tool that prioritizes user peace of mind and safety, ensuring that one bad actor doesn't ruin the experience for the entire group.

Design Goals

  • Empower the Host

    Create a clear, "one-tap" path to remove specific participants.

  • Ensure Scalability

    Design a solution that works across iPhone, iPad, and Mac ecosystems.

  • Uphold Simplicity

    Deliver a powerful utility that feels like it has always been part of the FaceTime DNA.

The initial idea for this project wasn't born in a design sprint, but during a casual FaceTime call with friends. This question prompted an initial Google search to see if the feature was even possible.

design & solution

I explored both the "vote-to-kick" or "any-participant-removes" concept similar to ones in video games and WhatsApp was considered, however, with discussing with my friends they all agreed a host-led removal model is the best solution.

It aligns best with Apple's design philosophy by creating a clear, simple line of responsibility and preventing the potential chaos. The "host" is the user who initiated the Group FaceTime call.

User flow & UI design:

The experience is broken into two distinct flows: one for the user performing the removal, and one for the participant who is removed.

Flow for the User (Removing a Participant):

1. The host taps the screen to show all participant tiles.
2. A small 'x' button is visible in the corner of each participant's tile.
3. The host clicks the 'x' button on the tile of the participant they wish to remove.
4. A system prompt appears, asking: "Remove [Contact Name] from FaceTime?"
5. The host confirms the action by tapping "OK" or "Cancel". The removed participant's tile disappears, and the FaceTime screen grid adjusts seamlessly to show only the remaining users.
6. The call continues without interruption.

Flow for the Removed Participant:

1. The participant is immediately and smoothly removed from the FaceTime call.
2. They are automatically taken to the Messages group chat where the FaceTime call was initiated. This provides immediate context for what happened.
3. A pop-up message appears over the chat, stating: "You've been removed from the call."
4. The language is direct but neutral. Within the chat, the FaceTime call banner at the top now appears inactive or the "Join" button is disabled for them, visually confirming they can no longer rejoin the active call.
